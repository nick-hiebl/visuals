This demonstration is of Braitenberg Vehicles, which are a concept first described by Valentino Braitenberg in terms of building intelligent-seeming robots from very simple logical parts. It's often used as a demonstration of how complex or intelligent behaviour can arise from basic reactive agents. Reactive agents use very simple logic with no memory, planning or model of the world in order to determine their actions. These are typically considered to be very limited in their behaviour and ineffective at solving any problems, however this is a demonstration of how they can produce seemingly intelligent actions.

The different agents can be described with the following behaviours:

  • Love or Seek
  • Aggression or Hatred
  • Fear or Hide
  • Explore or Observe
